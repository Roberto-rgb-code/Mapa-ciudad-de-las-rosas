• A
      Guadalajara Cathedral
      Seat of the Archdiocese, main Catholic temple, crypts of archbishops and notable religious images.
    • B
      Government Palace
      Original construction 1650, rebuilt after earthquake; Palace of the Royal Audiencia.
    • C
      Rotonda of Illustrious Jaliscienses
      Built 1952: 17 fluted columns, ceremonial brazier, 98 niches honoring notable people from Jalisco.
    • D
      Degollado Theatre
      Inaugurated 1866, neoclassical; home to Jalisco Philharmonic Orchestra, capacity 1027.
    • E
      Regional Museum
      From military barracks (1810) to public library (1857), Regional Museum of Guadalajara since 1976.
    • F
      Cabañas Museum
      UNESCO World Heritage; designed by Manuel Tolsá, frescoes by José Clemente Orozco.
    • G
      San Juan de Dios Market
      Largest covered market in Latin America, inaugurated 1958, on the San Juan de Dios River.
    • H
      Minerva
      Iconic monument (1956) by Joaquín Arias; Roman goddess Minerva, circular fountain.
    • I
      The Guadalajara Arches
      Along Av. Vallarta (1939-1942), by Aurelio Aceves; 400th anniversary of the city.
